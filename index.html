Travel & Directions Getting There

A few notes to make getting to Catawba a little easier.

By Car

Twin Oast Brewing is just outside Port Clinton, Ohio. If you plug “Twin Oast Brewing” into your GPS, it should take you right there. Give yourself a little extra time in case of summer traffic.

Nearest Airports

  • Cleveland Hopkins (CLE): About 1.5–2 hours by car, depending on traffic.
  • Detroit Metro (DTW): About 2 hours by car.
  • Toledo Express (TOL): Smaller airport, roughly 1–1.5 hours away.

Parking & Rideshare

There is on-site parking at Twin Oast. If you’re staying at a nearby hotel, you may be able to use the Twin Oast shuttle or a rideshare service—just check availability in advance, especially late at night.

Dress Code What To Wear

We’d describe our dress code as fun formal.

That means you’re welcome in dresses, jumpsuits, suits, dressy separates, or whatever feels festive and comfortable. Please avoid anything you’d wear to the gym or a beach day.

Have fun with color and personality—if it glows under a black light or looks great on a dance floor, even better. Comfortable shoes are encouraged; the venue is outside.

FAQ Good To Know

  • Are kids invited?

    They are welcome but not required! The venue has a large area to run around and play, but if you want to come by yourself we're ok with that too!

  • What about dietary restrictions?

    We will have vegetarian and gluten free options available at the food truck we're providing. The venue also has a full restaurant for a secondary option.

  • Will there be alcohol?

    Yes! Twin Oast is a brewery, so there will be great beer along with other drink options. Please plan a safe way to get back to your hotel.

  • Can we take photos / post on social?

    Please feel free to take photos and share them! We just ask that you stay present during the ceremony and avoid blocking the photographer.

  • Is everything at the same location?

    The ceremony and reception are both at Twin Oast Brewing. The day-afterparty will be at Twin Star Bowling Alley in Port Clinton.
